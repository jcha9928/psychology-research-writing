# Template 4.3: Reproducibility Checklist Worksheet

**Purpose**: Systematic scoring of 6 critical reproducibility elements (0-5 scale each, 30 points total)

**Use in Workshop**: Individual self-assessment (15min) → Peer cross-check (10min) → Improvement planning (5min)

**Target**: Achieve ≥24/30 (80%) reproducibility score for top-tier journal submission

---

## Checklist Philosophy

**"Can another researcher in your field replicate your study exactly using only your Methods section?"**

If the answer is NO → Reproducibility gap → Rejection risk

## Workshop Facilitation Guide

### Setup (Before Class - 10 minutes)

1. **Copy Figma Template**
   - Duplicate checklist worksheet canvas
   - Create individual assessment zones (6-12)
   - Generate sharing link

2. **Prepare Scoring Rubric**
   - Verify all 6 elements visible in left panel
   - Check good/bad examples are clear
   - Ensure 0-5 scoring criteria unambiguous

3. **Student Preparation**
   - Students should bring their current Methods section (digital copy ready to paste)
   - Brief instruction email: "Come prepared to self-assess your Methods reproducibility"

### During Workshop

#### Phase 1: Self-Assessment (15 minutes)

**Instructor Script**:
> "지금부터 15분간 자신의 Methods 섹션을 6가지 요소로 평가합니다.
>
> **각 element마다**:
> 1. '✅ What I HAVE' 체크 (현재 Methods에 있는 것)
> 2. 0-5 점수 매기기 (왼쪽 패널 기준 참고)
> 3. ⚠️ GAPS 식별 (빠진 것)
> 4. 📝 IMPROVEMENT PLAN 작성 (어떻게 추가할지)
>
> **목표**: 30점 만점에 몇 점인지 정직하게 평가!
>
> **Tip**: 보수적으로 평가하세요. 애매하면 낮은 점수.
> Nature 리뷰어는 관대하지 않습니다!"

**Monitoring**:
- Watch for generous self-scoring → Remind of conservative standard
- Look for common gaps across students → Note for debrief
- React to honest low scores with encouragement: "Good! Honest assessment = first step to improvement"

#### Phase 2: Peer Cross-Check (10 minutes)

**Instructor Script**:
> "이제 10분간 동료 1명의 평가를 cross-check합니다.
>
> **Pairing**: 학생A→B, B→C, C→D, ...
>
> **할 일**:
> 1. 동료의 '✅ What I HAVE' 읽기
> 2. 점수가 맞는지 확인 (너무 후하거나 박하지 않은지)
> 3. Figma comment (C키)로 피드백
>    - ✅ Confirmed: 동의하는 점수
>    - ⚠️ Adjustment: 조정 필요 (근거 포함)
>    - 💡 Additional gap: 본인이 못 본 gap
>    - 🎯 Priority: 가장 먼저 고칠 것
>
> **목표**: 객관적 평가 + 구체적 개선 제안"

**Facilitation**:
- Encourage specific feedback (not "looks good")
- Model constructive adjustment: "@학생B Element 3 is 3/5 not 4/5 - missing per-step timing"
- Highlight valuable peer catches: "학생C spotted missing assumption testing - excellent!"

#### Phase 3: Improvement Planning (5 minutes)

**Instructor Script**:
> "마지막 5분은 개선 계획입니다.
>
> **질문**:
> 1. Total score가 몇 점? (목표 24/30 이상)
> 2. 점수가 낮은 element는? (0-2점 → 우선 고칠 것)
> 3. Top 3 priority improvements는?
>
> **Strategic tip**:
> - Quick wins: Element 1 (Participants) - 정보 추가만 하면 됨
> - High impact: Element 6 (Analysis) - 리뷰어가 가장 주목
> - Time-intensive: Element 3 (Procedure) - 전체 재작성 필요할 수도
>
> Homework에서 최소 2개 element는 4→5점으로 올리세요!"

## Common Issues & Solutions

**Issue: "I scored myself 4/5 on everything, but my Methods is clearly <20/30"**
Solution: Instructor intervention - model conservative scoring: "If ANY of the 6 sub-items missing → max 3/5"

**Issue: "Element 6 (Analysis) is too technical, I don't understand scoring criteria"**
Solution: Simplify - "Do you report: preprocessing steps? Exclusion criteria? Software version? Assumptions testing? Code link? Missing >2 → ≤2/5"

**Issue: "Peer said my score is too low, but I followed rubric strictly"**
Solution: Peer may be too generous - instructor adjudicates: "Conservative scoring is correct for top-tier standards"

**Issue: "My total is 12/30 - does this mean my Methods is terrible?"**
Solution: Reframe - "No, it means you have clear path to improvement! Focus on 3 lowest elements, easy to reach 24/30"
